10 Easy Whiskey Cocktail Recipes

Whiskey is a versatile spirit that can be used to create a variety of delicious cocktails. Here are 10 easy whiskey cocktail recipes that you can try at home:

1. Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned is a cocktail made by muddling sugar with bitters then adding alcohol, such as whiskey or brandy, and a twist of citrus rind. It is traditionally served in a short, round, 8--12 US fl oz (240--350 ml) tumbler-like glass, which is called an Old Fashioned glass, named after the drink.

3. Rob Roy

The Rob Roy is a cocktail consisting primarily of whisky and vermouth, created in 1894 by a bartender at the Waldorf Astoria in Manhattan, New York City.

4. Irish Buck Cocktail

5. Revolver

The Revolver is a modern classic cocktail that combines the rich flavors of bourbon with the boldness of coffee liqueur and the aromatic complexity of orange bitters. It's a perfect drink for those who enjoy a balance of sweetness, bitterness, and warmth in their cocktails.

6. Matcha Highball

The Matcha Highball is a refreshing and unique cocktail that combines the earthy flavors of matcha green tea with the smoothness of whiskey and the effervescence of soda water. It's a perfect drink for those who enjoy a balance of bitterness, sweetness, and a hint of umami in their cocktails.

7. Manhattan

The Manhattan has managed to remain one of the most popular cocktails in the world for well over a century. A combination of whiskey, sweet vermouth, bitters, and a maraschino cherry garnish, this drink embodies the classic, spirit-forward, and aromatic school of cocktails.

9. Black Manhattan

A modern twist on the classic Manhattan cocktail, the Black Manhattan replaces the traditional sweet vermouth with Amaro Averna, an Italian herbal liqueur that adds a rich, complex flavor to the drink. This cocktail is perfect for those who enjoy a bold and sophisticated drink with a hint of bitterness.

10. Paper Plane

The Paper Plane is a modern cocktail that has gained popularity for its balanced flavors and simplicity. It combines equal parts bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, and fresh lemon juice, resulting in a drink that is both refreshing and complex. The cocktail was created by bartender Sam Ross in 2007 and has since become a favorite among cocktail enthusiasts.
